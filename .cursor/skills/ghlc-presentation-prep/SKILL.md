## Central Thesis

**Can AI-assisted medical imaging help expand access to diagnostic healthcare in underserved communities?**

The fracture detection CNN is one example; the presentation is a global health discussion.

## Slide Checklist

### Template bookends (mandatory — do not remove)

- [ ] Title: presentation title, name, school, **recording date**
- [ ] Introduction (~1 min): grade, hometown, school, extracurriculars, interests/future plans, project hook
- [ ] References: all sources and image/figure citations from [`docs/references/bibliography.md`](../../docs/references/bibliography.md)
- [ ] Acknowledgements: mentors, contributors, GHLC, audience
- [ ] Thank You: template slide
- [ ] Contact: name, email, LinkedIn — **no social media**

### Content slides (insert between Introduction and References)

- [ ] Background (~2 min): global health relevance, radiologist shortage, delayed diagnosis, rural/low-resource impact
- [ ] Body (~2–3 min): imaging overview, CNN fracture project, results in plain language, 1–2 charts
- [ ] Future apps (~0.5 min): TB, pneumonia, breast cancer, diabetic retinopathy
- [ ] Limitations (30–45 sec): bias, FP/FN, validation
- [ ] Conclusion (~2 min): AI augments doctors; responsible AI; personal reflection; call to action or question
- [ ] Deployment diagram from DEPLOYMENT_RESEARCH.md

## Speaker Notes

- Target ~960 words for spoken sections (~120 words/min × 8 min)
